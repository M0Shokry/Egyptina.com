Luxor

Luxor is a city in Upper Egypt, located on the east bank of the Nile River. It is a
unique destination for history and archaeology enthusiasts and is considered one of the
most important heritage sites in the world, as it contains nearly one-third of the
world’s monuments.

• Hot Air Balloon Ride:

•One of the most thrilling activities in Luxor is a hot air balloon ride. As you ascend over the city, you will witness breathtaking aerial views of iconic landmarks. The ride lasts between 30 to 45 minutes, reaching an altitude of up to 1,500 feet. Prices vary depending on the timing and tour operator, typically ranging between 700 to 1,200 EGP per person.

Felucca Boat Ride

A felucca ride offers a chance to enjoy the beauty of the Nile and the scenic landscapes of
Luxor. Whether in the sunny afternoon or during the peaceful sunset, it is an unforgettable
experience.

• Duration: Usually 1 to 3 hours, depending on your preference.
• Prices: Renting a felucca costs 100–150 EGP per hour, or 300–450 EGP for 3 hours,
depending on the demand.

Camel Riding

Exploring Luxor on the back of a camel is a unique and authentic way to experience the desert landscapes and ancient ruins.

• Duration: Between 1 to 3 hours, as per the tourist’s choice.
• Prices:

o 1-hour ride: 100–150 EGP.
o 3-hour tour: Includes visits to historical sites and traditional villages, costing around 100 USD.

Karnak Temple Complex

Located on the east bank of the Nile, Karnak Temple is one of the most famous sites in Luxor,
featuring a vast religious complex built by multiple pharaohs over centuries.

• Highlights of Karnak Temple

• Hypostyle Hall: A grand hall with 134 massive columns, covered in hieroglyphics
depicting historical events and pharaohs.

• Avenue of Sphinxes: A 2.7 km-long pathway that once connected Karnak Temple to
Luxor Temple. It has been restored into a world-class open-air museum featuring
sound and light shows.

• The Sacred Lake: Dug during the reign of Thutmose III, the lake remains intact for
over 3,000 years, with no changes in its water levels. .

Valley of the Kings

• The final resting place of the pharaohs of the 18th, 19th, and 20th Dynasties (~1550
1069 BCE).
• The valley holds over 60 tombs, carved into the cliffs.
• Tombs are adorned with intricate wall paintings illustrating the pharaoh’s journey to the
afterlife, based on ancient Egyptian beliefs.
• The complex architectural design helped safeguard royal treasures and prevent tomb
robberies.

Luxor Museum

Located along the Nile Corniche, this museum showcases priceless artifacts from Luxor’s
temples and tombs, including statues of kings, gods, and relics from various dynasties.

• First Floor: Features rare artifacts, including the statue of Amenhotep III, the statue of
Amun, and an exquisite schist statue of Thutmose III.

• Upper Floor: Displays artifacts of Akhenaten, along with limestone reliefs and a
collection from the Luxor Temple Cachette.

• Includes exhibits from the tomb of King Tutankhamun, discovered in 1922 in the
Valley of the Kings.

Mortuary Temple of Hatshepsut (Deir el-Bahari)

Located beneath a high cliff on the west bank of the Nile, this magnificent temple was built by Queen Hatshepsut in honor of Amun-Ra (the Sun God).
