Sharm El-Sheikh

Sharm El-Sheikh is a tourist city located at the meeting point of the Gulfs of Aqaba and Suez on
the Red Sea coast. It covers an area of 480 km² and has a population of 35,000. It is the largest
city in the South Sinai Governorate.

• Ras Mohammed Reserve:

• Ras Mohammed Reserve enjoys great international fame and has been ranked as the second most important nature reserve in the world. It is considered one of the most beautiful tourist destinations in Sharm El-Sheikh. The reserve features several rare natural landmarks, such as the rocky wall known as "Ras Za’atar," the stone gate, the Hidden Bay, the Earthquake Crack, and the meeting point of the two seas.

Al-Sahaba Mosque

Al-Sahaba Mosque is one of the most important tourist attractions in Sharm El-Sheikh and was recently constructed. It is the second-largest mosque in the city and is distinguished by its towering structure, two high minarets, a spacious courtyard, and Ottoman-style decorations adorning all its walls and façades. Additionally, Al-Sahaba Mosque is characterized by its brick-red color, while its courtyard is decorated with Islamic inscriptions and white marble. This makes it one of the most beautiful places in Sharm El-Sheikh, where visitors can enjoy a peaceful atmosphere and take stunning photographs..

Shark’s Bay

The name suggests that this area was once home to sharks, but that is a thing of the past. Today,
it has become a hub for luxury resorts and one of the most stunning tourist destinations in
Sharm El-Sheikh.Visitors to Shark’s Bay can enjoy a variety of recreational activities, such as
swimming, snorkeling, and beach games.

Location:

At the entrance of Concorde Hotel, Sheikh Zayed Street, Sharm El-Sheikh

The Blue Hole

The Blue Hole is a large diving sinkhole located on the eastern coast of Dahab, about 100 km
north of Sharm El-Sheikh. It consists of a deep opening called the Saddle, followed by a long
tunnel known as the Arch, leading to an open circular exit into the sea, which reaches a depth
of approximately 133 meters.

Top Activities at the Blue Hole: Learn professional diving at one of the 22 internationally
recognized diving centers. Enjoy the vibrant coral reefs and their stunning colors on both
sides of the hole.
