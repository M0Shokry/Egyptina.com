Alexandria

Alexandria is Egypt’s second capital and was its ancient capital. It is the country's largest city on the Mediterranean coast and is home to many remarkable landmarks.

• the Bibliotheca Alexandrina

The New Library of Alexandria, also known as the Bibliotheca Alexandrina, is a revival of the ancient
Library of Alexandria, which was the largest library of its time. This grand project was undertaken by
Egypt in collaboration with the United Nations, reconstructing the library near its original site in the
Shatby district of Alexandria. The modern library was officially inaugurated on October 16, 2002, in a
globally recognized event.

Kom El Shoqafa Tombs

Kom El Shoqafa is one of Alexandria’s most significant Roman-era burial sites, located in
the Karmouz district in the western part of the city. The name "Kom El Shoqafa" is
derived from the ancient Greek name "Locus Kerameikos," referring to the area’s historibrcal
association with pottery shards. The site was originally part of Rakotis, the ancient
Pharaonic settlement that later became Alexandria. This name is mentioned in a
hieroglyphic inscription from the reign of Ptolemy I, recorded as Ra-Gadit.

•Discovery & Historical Significance

Excavations in the Kom El Shoqafa area began in 1892, but the tombs were accidentally
discovered in 1900 when a hole was found in the burial chamber’s ceiling.

The tomb is of the "Catacomb" style, a type of underground burial site common in the first
three centuries AD. The Kom El Shoqafa Catacombs are the most significant burial complex in
the area, and their architectural layout resembles the early Christian catacombs in Rome.
The term "Catacombs" refers to underground cemeteries carved into the bedrock.

Qaitbay Citadel

Qaitbay Citadel is one of Alexandria’s most famous landmarks, located in the Eastern Harbor
within the historic El-Gomrok district, one of the city’s oldest neighborhoods.Built over 500
years ago, the fortress stands on the ruins of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria, one of the
Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Surrounding AttractionsThe area around the citadel
offers many tourist activities, including: A scenic walk along the Corniche. Relaxing by the
sea. Enjoying local specialties such as fresh juices, ice cream, and seafood in the Bahary
district.

Royal Jewelry Museum

The Royal Jewelry Museum showcases the exquisite jewelry collections of Egypt’s former
royal families. It is located in Alexandria, in the Zizinia district, within a magnificent palace
built in 1919.

Originally owned by Princess Fatma Al-Zahraa, a member of the royal family, the palace itself
is an architectural masterpiece, covering 4,185 square meters and designed in 19th-century
European style, with lavish artistic decorations adorning its interiors.

•istory & Collection

The palace was transformed into the Royal Jewelry Museum in 1986. It houses a vast
collection of royal gold artifacts and jewelry from the Muhammad Ali dynasty (1805-1952).
The collection includes rare treasures from Muhammad Ali Pasha to King Farouk I. These
royal possessions were confiscated after the July 1952 Revolution and later registered as a
national museum in 1999.Today, the museum holds an impressive collection of 11,500 pieces..
