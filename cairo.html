Cairo:

The Historical and Contemporary Capital of Egypt Cairo is one of the oldest and largest cities in the world, blending a rich history with modern development. By 2025, it has become an urban model that reflects a balance between cultural heritage and modern progress. Cairo offers a perfect mix of activities that combine history, entertainment, and culture.

"Pyramids & Sphinx (Giza)"

One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, this site includes the Great Pyramid of Khufu, as well as the pyramids of Khafre and Menkaure. The Sphinx, a mythical statue, symbolizes strength and wisdom.

•Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon.
• Recommended Visit Duration: 3 hours is sufficient to explore the site.
• Activities: Visiting the pyramids and the Sphinx, camel or horseback riding, and exploring the Solar Boat Museum.
• Tips: Wear comfortable clothing and sports shoes, avoid climbing the pyramids, and hire a tour guide if possible.

"Saqqara Site"

Saqqara is home to the Step Pyramid of King Djoser and ancient tombs dating back to the Pharaonic era.

•Important Information •

Main Attractions: The Step Pyramid of Djoser (the oldest pyramid in Egypt) and tombs adorned with stunning carvings.

1• Recommended Visit Duration: 2 hours.

2• • Best Time to Visit: Early morning

''Salah aldin Citadel''

The citadel is home to the Mosque of Muhammad Ali, with its towering minarets and Ottoman-style architecture, offering a panoramic view of Cairo.

•Main Attractions Inside the Citadel

• Mosque of Muhammad Ali: One of the most famous historic mosques.
• Military Museum: Showcasing the history of the Egyptian army.

• Visitor Guidelines

• Location: The Citadel area, Cairo.

• Accessibility: Easily reachable by car.

• Tip: Enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of Cairo from the top of the citadel

''The Hanging Church''

One of Egypt’s oldest churches, located in Old Cairo, built atop the gate of the Babylon Fortress. Built in the 4th century AD over two towers of the Roman Fortress (Babylon Fortress). The church is known for its suspended design, constructed above the remains of the fortress, giving it its name. Situated in the Religious Complex area in Old Cairo. Free entry for all visitors

"Visitor Information"

• Main Attractions:

o Ancient Christian icons, some dating back to the 8th century AD.

o Unique wooden-roof architecture resembling Noah’s Ark.

o Beautifully decorated altars and halls with intricate details.

•Visiting Hours:

• • Open daily from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

• Closed on special religious holidays.

•Notable Activities:

• Exploring the church’s distinctive architecture and rare icons.

• Visiting nearby landmarks such as St. George Church and the Coptic Museum.

''Khan El Khalili Street in Cairo''

Khan El Khalili is one of the oldest and most famous historical markets in Cairo, located in the heart of the old city near the Al-Hussein area. The street is known for its authentic oriental atmosphere, where the scent of incense and spices blends with the voices of vendors and the movement of tourists, making it one of the most important tourist destinations in Egypt.

•History of Khan El Khalili

Khan El Khalili dates back to the 14th century when it was built by Prince Jaharkas Al-Khalili during the Mamluk era as a major trading market in Cairo. Over the centuries, the market has remained a center for traditional crafts and commerce, preserving its authenticity to this day.

What Can You Find in Khan El Khalili?

The market offers a wide variety of shops selling traditional Egyptian products, such as:

• Handicrafts: Copperware, mother-of-pearl inlaid wood, pottery, and carpets.

• Jewelry and Accessories: Silver, gold, and handmade ornaments.

• Spices and Perfumes: A vast selection of aromatic spices, essential oils, and incense.

• Traditional Clothing: Galabiyas, scarves, and embroidered fabrics.

• Antiques and Souvenirs: Papyrus paintings, statues, and Pharaonic replicas.

•A Unique Cultural Experience

Visiting Khan El Khalili is not just about shopping; it's an immersive cultural experience. Tourists and locals alike enjoy sipping Egyptian tea or coffee while soaking in the market’s vibrant energy.

Whether you're looking for unique souvenirs or just want to explore Cairo's rich history, Khan El Khalili is a must-visit destination!
