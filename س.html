Siwa Oasis

Siwa oasis is one of Egypt's most famous oases, located in the Western Desert. It is renowned
for its breathtaking landscapes, unique Amazigh (Berber) culture, and rich history that
dates back thousands of years. Siwa is a perfect destination for travelers seeking tranquility,
relaxation, and a deep connection with nature.

Siwa Oasis is a desert gem that blends history, nature, and unique culture. It is an ideal
destination for tourists seeking a distinct experience and an unforgettable adventure.

• Language: The local community speaks Siwi, an ancient Amazigh (Berber) language.
• Customs & Traditions: Siwa is famous for traditional weddings and distinctive local
attire.
• Cuisine: The oasis is known for Siwan couscous, dates, and olives.

Historical Landmarks

1. Temple of Amun (Oracle Temple)

o Dates back to the Pharaonic era and was famously visited by Alexander the Great.

2. Um Ubayda Temple

o Dates back to the Pharaonic era and was famously visited by Alexander the Great.

Um Ubayda Temple o Located near the Temple of Amun, dating back to the 6th century BC.

3. Mountain of the Dead (Jebel Dakrour)

o Contains ancient Pharaonic tombs and is one of Siwa’s most significant
archaeological sites.

4. Shali Fortress

o An ancient mudbrick fortress that once served as a refuge for the local population.

Natural Attractions

1. Siwa Lakes

o Includes Fatnas Lake and Zaytun Lake, offering perfect spots for relaxation and
scenic beauty.
o Entry fee: Free for all.

2. Natural Springs

o Cleopatra’s Spring and Ain Al-Arais are natural hot springs used for bathing and
therapy.
o Entry fee:Free for all.

3. Sand Dunes

o Visitors can enjoy desert safaris, camel rides, and quad biking.

4. Siwa Grand Oasis

o Famous for its palm groves and olive orchards, a great place to explore and enjoy
nature.
o Entry fee: Free for all.

. Tourist Activities

1. Therapeutic Tourism

o Siwa is known for its natural clay and sulfurous waters, used in treating skin
diseases and rheumatism.
o Entry fee: Free for all.

2. Desert Safari Tours

o Explore the surrounding desert through thrilling safari adventures.
o Entry fee:Free for all.

3. Shopping

o Visitors can buy local products such as olives, dates, and traditional
handicrafts.
